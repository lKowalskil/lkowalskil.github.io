01 · THE PREMISE

A fixed watering day is convenient. Your plants did not agree to it.

“Water every Sunday” is easy to remember, but the plant’s needs can shift as light, temperature, growth, pot size, and soil conditions change. A reminder is most useful when it brings the plant back to your attention and asks for a quick check.

That distinction matters: the reminder schedules the observation. The condition of the plant and growing medium decides the action. Sometimes the correct response to a watering reminder is “not today.”

Important: this is a routine for staying attentive, not species-specific horticultural advice. Follow the care needs of the actual plant and adjust when its condition tells you the routine is wrong.

02 · THE ROUTINE

Make every reminder a four-step check.

CHECK—01 Look

Notice drooping, discoloration, new growth, and whether the plant has shifted toward light.

CHECK—02 Check the growing medium

Use the method appropriate for that plant and pot instead of relying only on elapsed days.

CHECK—03 Act—or defer

Water when the plant needs it. Otherwise move the reminder forward without guilt.

CHECK—04 Record

Log what you did so the next decision has context rather than depending on memory.

This routine takes less time than rescuing a plant after several weeks of inconsistent care. It also works for fertilizer, misting, rotation, and repotting checks—each on its own rhythm.

03 · ORGANIZATION

Group plants by conditions, not just by room.

Two plants can sit in the same room and experience very different light. A shelf near a window, a shaded corner, and a warm sill are separate care contexts. Grouping plants into light-aware zones makes a care list easier to interpret.

  • Name the place clearly: “East window” is more useful than “Living room.”
  • Keep shared pots visible: plants sharing space may still have incompatible needs.
  • Review after a move: a changed location can make the previous reminder interval less useful.
  • Expect seasons to matter: revisit schedules instead of assuming the first setup will remain correct forever.

04 · FEEDBACK

Your care history is more valuable than a perfect first schedule.

A schedule is a starting hypothesis. The history of checks, watering, fertilizing, and visible changes helps you refine it. If you repeatedly defer a reminder, the interval may be too short. If a plant regularly shows stress before the reminder, the routine or placement deserves attention.

Review one week ahead rather than only reacting to today. A short forecast lets you combine care tasks, notice crowded days, and avoid forgetting a plant when the week becomes busy.

05 · QUICK ANSWERS

Plant watering reminders, briefly.

Should a reminder always mean “water now”?

No. Treat it as a prompt to inspect the plant and growing medium, then decide.

Can Mossby track more than watering?

Can I organize plants by location?

Yes. Mossby groups plants into zones and pots, including information about light.

Does the core app work offline?

Yes. Your plant list, photos, schedules, and care history are designed to stay on your device.
