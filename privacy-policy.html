Privacy Policy

Effective date: 26 June 2026  ·  Last updated: 26 June 2026

This Privacy Policy explains how Mossby (the “App”), published by Nyxelium (“we”, “us”, “our”), handles information when you use the App. Mossby is a plant-care and watering-reminder app available on Google Play (package com.nyxelium.mossby).

The short version
  • Your plants, photos, schedules and settings are stored only on your device. We don’t run a server that collects them, and we can’t see them.
  • We do not sell your personal data.
  • If you use the free version, third-party advertising (Google AdMob) and optional analytics (Google Firebase) may collect device and usage data as described below.
  • A paid subscription removes ads. Payments are processed by Google Play; we never see your card details.
  • You can opt out of analytics in the App, and control ad personalization through your device and on-screen consent.

1. Information stored on your device

Mossby is designed to work offline. The following stays on your device and is not transmitted to us:

  • Plant & garden data — the plants, zones, pots, care tasks, watering history and streaks you create.
  • Photos — any photo you attach to a plant. Photos are copied into the App’s private storage and are not uploaded to us or any third party.
  • Preferences — theme, language, notification and analytics settings.

This data is removed when you delete the App or clear its storage. Because it never leaves your device, we cannot retrieve or restore it for you.

2. Information collected by third parties

To offer a free, ad-supported tier and to keep the App stable, we use the trusted third-party services below. These services may collect information directly from your device under their own privacy policies.

ServicePurposeExamples of data it may process
Google AdMob

Analytics (Firebase) is off by default and only runs if you turn on “Share usage data” in the App’s Privacy settings. Advertising data is processed only in the free version and subject to your consent choices (see §5).

3. How we use information

  • To provide the App’s core features (reminders, care history, illustrations).
  • To display and measure advertising in the free version.
  • To process and restore your subscription, and remove ads for subscribers.
  • To diagnose crashes and improve performance and features (only with analytics enabled).
  • To comply with legal obligations and prevent fraud or abuse.

4. Legal bases (EEA / UK users)

Where the EU/UK GDPR applies, we rely on:

  • Consent — for personalized ads and for optional usage analytics. You may withdraw consent at any time.
  • Contract — to provide the App and process subscriptions you request.
  • Legitimate interests — for non-personalized ads, basic crash diagnostics, security and fraud prevention, balanced against your rights.

5. Advertising & your choices

The free version of Mossby shows ads via Google AdMob. Depending on your region and consent:

  • EEA, UK & Switzerland: we show a consent message (Google’s User Messaging Platform) before serving personalized ads. You can change your choice later from the App’s Privacy settings.
  • Reset or limit your Advertising ID: Android → Settings → Privacy → Ads (or Google → Ads), where you can reset the ID or turn off ad personalization.
  • Remove ads entirely: subscribe to the paid plan.

Learn how Google uses data from apps that use its services: policies.google.com/technologies/partner-sites.

6. Device permissions

  • Camera / Photos — only to let you attach a photo to a plant; used at the moment you pick or take a photo. Photos stay on your device.
  • Notifications — to deliver watering and care reminders you schedule. You can disable them in your device settings.

The App requests internet access for ads, analytics and subscription validation in the free/subscribed flows described above.

7. Data sharing

We do not sell your personal information and we do not share it with third parties for their own marketing. Information is processed only by the service providers named in §2, acting on our behalf or as independent controllers under their own policies, and where required by law.

8. Data retention

On-device data persists until you delete it or uninstall the App. Data held by Google services (advertising, analytics, billing) is retained according to Google’s policies. Crash and analytics data is typically retained for a limited period and in aggregated form.

9. Your rights

Depending on where you live (e.g. EEA/UK under GDPR, California under the CCPA/CPRA), you may have rights to access, correct, delete, or restrict processing of your personal data, to object to processing, to data portability, and to not be discriminated against for exercising these rights. Because most of your content lives only on your device, you can exercise access and deletion directly by viewing or removing it in the App. For data held by Google services, see Google’s policy. To make a request to us, contact us at the address below.

10. Children’s privacy

Mossby is intended for a general audience and is not directed to children under 13 (or the equivalent minimum age in your country). We do not knowingly collect personal data from children. Ads are configured for a general audience; if you believe a child has provided us personal data, please contact us so we can address it.

11. International data transfers

Our service providers may process data in countries other than yours, including the United States. Where required, such transfers rely on appropriate safeguards (such as the European Commission’s Standard Contractual Clauses).

12. Security

We rely on your device’s app-sandboxing to protect on-device data and on reputable providers (Google) for processed data. No method of storage or transmission is 100% secure, but we take reasonable measures to protect your information.

13. Changes to this policy

We may update this policy from time to time. We will revise the “Last updated” date above and, for material changes, provide notice in the App or on this page. Continued use after changes means you accept the updated policy.
